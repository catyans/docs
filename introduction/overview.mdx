## What is VINE?

Vine's vision is to democratize institutional investing. By combining rigorous research, AI, and Web3-native efficiency, VINE aims to break down financial barriers and connect fiat and on-chain assets through a single platform.

Today, the VINE API documents the firm's **AI-native BTC/ETH multi-strategy trading and allocation infrastructure**, including portfolio data, risk monitoring, webhook ingestion, reporting, and related developer workflows.

The platform is designed for:

- **EAMs** — Research, screening, and allocation workflows for managed client capital
- **Exchanges** — Strategy connectivity, reporting, and operational integrations
- **B2C Wealth Platforms** — Product distribution, portfolio visibility, and downstream tooling
- **Internal Trading & Research Teams** — Strategy operations, monitoring, and execution support

## Strategy Overview

VINE's trading and allocation stack is built around **BTC and ETH** as core assets, combining multiple low-correlation alpha sources under a single institutional workflow.

### Investment Principles

- **Single asset universe, multiple alpha dimensions** — Concentrated exposure to BTC and ETH, but diversified through distinct research and signal layers rather than superficial product packaging.
- **AI-native decision making** — AI agents participate directly in news interpretation, event classification, and signal generation.
- **Execution as alpha** — Slippage, market impact, and implementation shortfall are treated as part of the research feedback loop.
- **Tail-risk first** — The system prioritizes worst-case scenario quantification and drawdown control before return optimization.

### Strategy Modules

| Module | Core Logic |
| --- | --- |
| **News / Event Guard** | Uses LLMs to classify regulatory, security, ETF, and macro events, then calibrates impact decay using historical analogs. |
| **Macro Regime** | Uses rates, DXY, yield curve, VIX, and macro calendar events to classify `Risk-On`, `Transitional`, and `Risk-Off` environments. |
| **Vol Agent** | Targets IV-RV mean reversion, term-structure dislocations, and volatility regime opportunities derived from Deribit data. |

<Note>
  `Vol Agent` is planned for Phase 2. Phase 1 focuses on the `News + Macro` stack, with strategy weights evolving through backtests and live trading evidence.
</Note>

### Signal Construction

Signals are combined through **conviction weighting** and **time decay** before being translated into a target position.

```text
target_position = f(agent_weight, conviction, direction, decay_factor, regime_overlay)
```

### Portfolio Construction And Execution

- **Fractional Kelly** — Uses a conservative Kelly framework to balance capital growth with drawdown control.
- **Vol Targeting** — Dynamically adjusts position sizing toward a target volatility range.
- **Risk Budgeting** — Allocates risk using marginal contribution rather than simple notional sizing.
- **PMS → OMS → EMS separation** — Keeps portfolio logic, order logic, and execution logic cleanly separated.
- **TCA feedback loop** — Feeds implementation shortfall and execution quality back into signal thresholds and execution parameters.

### Risk Framework

| Layer | Scope |
| --- | --- |
| **L1 Agent Layer** | Per-trade stop limits, daily loss caps, low-conviction filtering, and volatility targeting constraints. |
| **L2 Supervisor Layer** | Factor overlap controls, EVT tail-risk monitoring, staged drawdown protocol, and cross-strategy correlation monitoring. |
| **L3 Hard Limits** | Max leverage, exchange exposure caps, margin utilization ceilings, and non-overridable trading limits. |
| **L4 Circuit Breaker** | Full de-risking under extreme drawdowns or system failures, with manual approval required for restart. |

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Signal Generation">
    AI agents (news analysis, macro regime, funding rate) generate directional signals with conviction scores and decay half-lives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Signal Combination">
    Signals are combined via weighted ensemble: `w = Σ(α_agent × conviction × direction × decay_factor)`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Portfolio Construction">
    Volatility targeting, regime overlay, and leverage constraints produce a target BTC position.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Execution & Risk">
    Orders pass through 4-layer risk checks before execution. TCA monitors slippage and implementation shortfall.
  </Step>
</Steps>
